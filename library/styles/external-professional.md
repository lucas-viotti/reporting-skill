# Style: External Professional

## Overview

Use for communication with external parties: recruiters, vendors, partners, contractors,
and anyone outside the organization. Maintains professional warmth while being clear
and direct. Avoids internal jargon and sensitive organizational context.

---

## Tone Characteristics

- Professional and warm — courteous without being over-familiar
- Clear and self-contained — no assumed internal context
- Direct about expectations — clear on timelines, asks, and constraints
- Privacy-conscious — no internal team names, project codenames, or org structure
- Appropriately brief — respects the external party's time

---

## Structural Patterns

Messages follow: **Context → Position/Ask → Next step**

- Open with brief context that is self-contained (assumes no prior knowledge)
- State your position or ask clearly
- End with a specific next step or call to action

For longer emails: use short paragraphs, not bullets (more formal register).

---

## Writing Rules

1. Never use internal codenames or project names visible externally (e.g., "Troy", team names)
2. Be specific about what you need without revealing internal constraints
3. Decline politely and firmly — no need to over-explain or apologize
4. Confirm mutual understanding: "To confirm, you're expecting X by Y"
5. Use "we" for company positions, "I" for personal preferences/decisions

---

## Language Patterns

### Preferred
- "Thank you for reaching out"
- "We're not currently exploring this, but I appreciate you thinking of us"
- "To confirm my understanding…"
- "The timeline we're working with is [date]"
- "Happy to connect if [specific condition]"

### Avoided
- Internal project names, team names, codenames
- Org chart references ("my skip", "the VP of X")
- Vague declines: "We'll keep you in mind" (when you won't)
- Overly casual: "Sure thing!" / "Totally!" — too informal for external parties
- Overly formal: "I humbly request…" — anachronistic

---

## Example Messages

**Recruiter decline:**
> Thanks for reaching out. I'm not actively exploring new opportunities right now, but I appreciate you thinking of me. Feel free to reach out again in [timeframe] if anything relevant comes up.

**Vendor inquiry response:**
> Thank you for the demo. We're evaluating a few options and will be in touch by [date] with a decision. In the meantime, could you send the pricing breakdown and integration documentation?

**Partnership clarification:**
> To confirm my understanding: you're proposing [X] in exchange for [Y], with delivery by [date]. Before we move forward, I need clarity on [specific open question]. Can you provide that by [date]?

**Setting expectations with a contractor:**
> Looking forward to working together. A few things to confirm before we kick off: deliverables are [X, Y], timeline is [dates], and the point of contact on our side is [name/role]. Let me know if you have questions.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- "As per our internal process…" — unnecessary and exposes internal detail
- Forwarding internal Slack threads or Confluence links to external parties
- Over-apologizing for declining: "I'm so sorry but unfortunately at this time we can't…"
- Vague positive signals to vendors you're not interested in: "We'll definitely keep this in mind!" — misleading
