# Style: Leadership Polite

## Overview

Use for requests to teams, diplomatic asks, nudging action, and situations requiring
firmness without friction. Maintains collaborative tone while being clear about what
is needed and when.

---

## Tone Characteristics

- Respectful and collaborative — acknowledges the other party's context
- Clear about the ask — no ambiguity about what is needed
- Firm on timelines — states deadlines without apology
- Non-confrontational — addresses issues without blame
- Concise — one ask per message; no lengthy justification

---

## Structural Patterns

Messages follow: **Context → Ask → Timeline** (or **Acknowledgment → Ask → Why it matters**)

- Open with brief context or acknowledgment (1 sentence max)
- State the ask clearly and specifically
- Provide timeline and/or reason why it matters
- Optional: offer help or next step

---

## Writing Rules

1. Never bury the ask — state it in the first 2 sentences
2. Give a concrete timeline ("by Thursday EOD", "before the sprint review") not vague ("soon", "when you can")
3. Acknowledge constraints when visible: "I know you're heads down on X…"
4. Avoid passive-aggressive phrasing: "It would be great if someone could…"
5. One ask per message — don't stack requests
6. **Positive reframe before the ask** when delivering difficult feedback — "frank and productive conversation" before the summary of failures
7. **Name ownership explicitly** — "I'd ask [Name] to own X" not "someone should handle X"
8. Persistent but gracious — "can you please humor me one last time" signals you're pushing back without being confrontational

---

## Language Patterns

### Preferred
- "Could you [specific ask] by [date]?"
- "I need [X] to unblock [Y] — would [date] work?"
- "Following up on [X] — still need [specific thing]"
- "Happy to jump on a call if easier"
- "No rush, but [deadline] is the latest this can wait"

### Avoided
- "Per my last message…" — passive aggressive
- "As discussed…" — implies blame
- "Can you please just…" — sounds exasperated
- "I was wondering if maybe you could…" — too hedged; buries the ask
- "When you get a chance" — too vague for anything with a deadline

---

## Example Messages

**Nudging for a decision:**
> Hey [name] — still need a decision on the design criteria before we can finalize the feature design. Teams are unblocked except for this. Could we lock it in by Thursday?

**Following up on a blocker:**
> Following up on the [team] alignment — I know the team is busy, but this is on the critical path for the release. If it helps, happy to set up a 30-min sync this week to resolve it.

**Post-meeting ask with named ownership:**
> Thanks — a frank and productive conversation. A few tactical next steps:
> 1. I'd ask [Name] to own the technical counterpart relationship going forward.
> 2. [Name] and [Name] to align on a weekly cadence — flag yellow items to me; red items escalate immediately.
> Please let me know if you have questions. Thanks.

**Requesting a review:**
> [Name], could you take a look at the updated roadmap before Friday? Just need a thumbs up or specific objections — trying to share with leadership by EOW.

**Diplomatic escalation notice:**
> Heads up: if we don't have alignment on X by Monday, we'll need to escalate to [lead] to keep the Beta timeline. Flagging early so we have time to resolve it without that step.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- "I've sent this 3 times already" — confrontational
- "This should have been done last week" — accusatory
- "I'll need to escalate if this isn't resolved" as an opener — threatening without context
- Excessive softening: "I know you're super busy and I totally understand if this takes time, but if you could maybe…" — dilutes the ask
